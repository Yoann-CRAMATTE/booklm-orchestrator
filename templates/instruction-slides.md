## Example (Filled)

```markdown
## SLIDES #1: Fundamentals of Decentralization

**Total Slides**: 8
**Chapters Covered**: Chapters 1-2
**Audience**: Master's students in Public Administration

**Slide Breakdown**:

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- Title: "Fundamentals of Decentralization"
- Subtitle: "Understanding France's Multi-Level Governance"
- Visual: Image of French administrative map

### Slide 2: What is Decentralization?
- Definition: Transfer of power from central to local government
- Historical context: France's shift from centralization
- Why it matters: Citizen representation, local responsiveness
- Visual: Timeline graphic (centralization → decentralization)

### Slide 3: Three Levels of Governance
- Communes (Local): 36,000+ municipalities
- Departments: 96 mid-level regions
- Regions: 18 large administrative areas
- Visual: Hierarchy diagram showing three levels

### Slide 4: Subsidiarity Principle
- Definition: Authority at the lowest appropriate level
- Benefits: Closer to citizens, better local knowledge
- Limitations: Need coordination for efficiency
- Visual: Concentric circles (local → national)

### Slide 5: Deconcentration vs Decentralization
- Deconcentration: Administrative delegation (same authority)
- Decentralization: Power transfer (different authority)
- Common confusion: These are often mixed up
- Visual: Comparison table or Venn diagram

### Slide 6-7: Powers by Level (examples)
- Slide 6: What communes handle (schools, local services)
- Slide 7: What regions handle (economic development, transport)
- Visual: Detailed breakdown table

### Slide 8: Summary & Next Steps
- Key takeaways: Multi-level governance in France
- Next segment will cover: Specific competencies & financing
- Discussion questions for audience engagement
- Visual: Infographic of key points

---

## COPY-PASTE SECTION FOR BOOKLM

Create slides covering French administrative decentralization (chapters 1-2). Each slide should:

1. **Slide on Decentralization**: Define as power transfer from central to local government. Show historical evolution.

2. **Slide on Three Levels**: Show communes (local), departments (mid-level), regions (large). Include examples.

3. **Slide on Subsidiarity**: Explain the principle. Show why it matters for governance.

4. **Comparison Slide**: Deconcentration vs Decentralization side-by-side. Clarify common confusion.

5. **Powers Distribution**: What each level handles (education, services, development).

**Style**: Clean, minimal text (3-5 bullets per slide), visual diagrams where possible.

**Audience**: Master's students preparing for public administration careers.

---
```

## Guidelines for Slides

### Design Principles
- **1 concept per slide minimum** (can do 2 related concepts)
- **3-5 bullet points maximum**
- **Visual element per slide** (diagram, chart, image)
- **Speaker notes** for detailed explanation

### Text Guidelines
- ✅ Bullet points, not paragraphs
- ✅ Key words/phrases only
- ✅ Nouns over verbs (action words in notes)
- ✅ Consistent style throughout

### Visuals
- ✅ Diagram for abstract concepts
- ✅ Chart for data/comparisons
- ✅ Image for real-world examples
- ✅ Placeholder descriptions if skill generates

### Speaker Notes
- Full explanation of slide content
- Real-world examples
- Potential audience questions
- Emphasis points

### Flow
- Slide 1: Title
- Slide 2: Roadmap/overview
- Slides 3-N: Concept content
- Final slide: Summary + next steps
